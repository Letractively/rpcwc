Now What....


GOD SENT HIS SON TO ACCOMPLISH THAT RESTORED RELATIONSHIP.


Everything that was needed for salvation and a restored relationship with God, which was impossible for us, God Himself did through the work of His Son, Jesus Christ. The whole Old Testament speaks of God's promise of salvation and presents pictures to help us understand what God would do on our behalf. Then in God's perfect timing:

"But when the time had fully come, God sent His Son, born of a woman, born under law, to redeem those under law, that we might receive the full rights of sons." (Galatians 4:4-5)

What exactly did Jesus do to reconcile sinners to God?


Jesus, the eternal Son of God42, became a real human being43 so that He could stand in the place of sinful human beings as their sinless representative44. This is what we celebrate at Christmas. As God incarnate (God in full humanity), Jesus was able to live a perfect sinless life of flawless obedience45 that we could not, yet pay the penalty of the law breaker that we deserved to suffer46.
In order to satisfy the justice of a holy God, Jesus gave His life as a sacrifice by dying on the cross as a sinless substitute47. This is what we celebrate at Good Friday. Jesus arrived at the cross with no sin of His own, and so was able to serve as a substitute for sinners. On the cross He atoned for48 (i.e., paid the price for, removing the guilt) the sins of those He came to save and He suffered the full wrath of God to satisfy the justice49 of God's holiness and make those who were the objects of His wrath50 pleasing to Him51.
And in vindication of His mission52 and validation of His victory53, God raised Jesus from the dead. This is what we celebrate at Easter. This resurrection means that Jesus' mission to redeem (pay the price for) a people for God was a success54. God had provided the way for forgiveness of sins, newness of life and reconciliation of sinners with Himself without compromising His character. In fact, God has provided no other way. As Jesus says, He is the way, the truth and the life; no one comes to the Father, except through Him 55.
What is so amazing in all this is that God did this though He was in no way compelled to. In answer to the question, "Who crucified Christ?," the ultimate answer of the Bible is: "For God so loved the world He gave His only begotten Son56;" "God made Him who knew no sin to be sin on our behalf57;" "It was the Lord's will to crush Him and cause Him to suffer58."

So you see that God did not just ignore sin of those He decided to save. Nor did He simply issue a pardon. No, God paid for the sins of His people by sending His Son to give His life a ransom59 for them and to provide the righteousness (perfect obedience) required by His law so that they could be acceptable to Him60.

The question now is, how can YOU be reconciled to God?
