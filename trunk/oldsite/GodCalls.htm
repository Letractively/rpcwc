GOD CALLS EVERYONE TO RETURN TO HIM.


The good news is that God has accomplished the salvation of His people through the work of His Son, Jesus Christ. That which we could never do, nor would we ever deserve, God did in Christ - reconciling people to Himself, not counting their sins against them61. Not that that sin was ignored; quite the contrary, it was met head on in all its ugliness and violence by the Son of God for His sheep. As the Good Shepherd, Jesus says He will not lose any the Father has given Him. Jesus says one other thing about His sheep:

"My sheep listen to My voice; I know them, and they follow me. I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish; no one can snatch them out of My hand." (John 10:27-28)

God's command is to turn to His Son, Jesus Christ, and follow Him. What is involved in following Christ? The Bible describes three proper responses to this Jesus who is Savior of sinners and Lord of life.


We are to Repent62. Repentance is not merely being sorry for something. It is turning from our sinful rebellion against God, out a deep awareness of our sin and grief63 over that sin because it dishonors and displeases God64. Repentance turns from any idea of being our own savior, thinking we can do something to earn salvation. It turns from being our own lord, rejecting God to follow the desires and agenda of self.
Not only are we to turn from, we are to turn to65. We are to turn from self to Jesus Christ66. We are to repent and believe on the Lord Jesus Christ. This is what the Bible calls "faith." Such faith knows the facts (about sin and where salvation is found), admits they are true, and places sole trust and confidence in God's provision for sinners - Jesus Christ67. Faith is not wishful thinking, but trust in an unseen reality68.
Since Jesus is Lord, we now are to live our lives in submission to Him69. We are to commit our way to Him, seeking Him and serving Him in all our ways70 because we are His71 - not that we either earn our acceptance by God or add to the work of Christ. But if God has given us new life to hear the voice of the Shepherd72, that new life will result in a new orientation to God73. A true change of heart by the Spirit of God will manifest itself in a change of life in new relationship to God74.
God's command is to repent (Acts 17:3075) and believe (1 John 3:2376). All of this is God's handiwork of love and power in our lives as He is the one to restore us to relationship with Himself, both for this life and the life to come77. Perhaps the most amazing thing is this: the relationship to which God brings us is to be His own adopted children78, heirs of eternal life79. While God commands that we turn to Christ, His promise is that all who acknowledge Jesus as the One He claimed to be and believe on Him are given the right to become children of God80.
