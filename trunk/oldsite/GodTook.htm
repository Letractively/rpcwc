GOD TOOK INITIATIVE TO RESTORE THAT RELATIONSHIP.


While we were without God and without hope in the world, separated from our Creator God in our sinfulness, unable to do anything to save ourselves from the judgment of God and consequences of our sinful rebellion, God took initiative. God, the Holy One sinned against, set His undeserved, unexpected and unmerited love on sinners to save them28. Notice God's initiative in saving His people from their sins:

"But God demonstrates His own love for us in this: While we were yet sinners..." (Romans 5:8)

"We were by nature objects of wrath. But because of His great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive..." (Ephesians 2:3-4)

God took this initiative not because His character compelled Him to or because He was constrained by anything in us29, but only because of His own decision30. The Bible expresses this in three ways:


God set His love on sinners in the face of their rebellion 31. In the Bible God's love is an act of His will to save32. God's just wrath is not the contrast to His love. Rather, God's just wrath is the context for His love33. If the dark and bleak backdrop of God's just wrath is removed, the brilliant glory and warmth of His love is dimmed. The teaching of the Bible is that "God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son that whoever believes on Him will not perish...34" Remove the "perish" (i.e., God's wrath), and the love doesn't make any sense.
To those who deserve condemnation and eternal punishment in satisfaction of His justice, God shows mercy35. Mercy does not give a person what he or she deserves. Mercy spares us justice. The Bible says, "But when the kindness and love of God our Savior appeared, He saved us, not because of righteous things we had done, but because of His mercy.36"
If mercy does not give us what we do deserve, grace 37 gives us what we do not deserve. To say that God's salvation is by grace means that it comes to us as a gift.  It is not something we can earn by our efforts at obeying God's law or by trying to make up for the sin we have done in the past. Salvation does not come by turning over a new leaf, but by being given a new heart, a new nature38. In fact, the Bible says that "it is by grace we are saved through faith, and that not of ourselves, it is a gift of God, not by good works.39"
As sinners we are unlovely and unlovable. Yet God set His love upon sinners such as us to provide salvation and to restore that broken relationship. But that begs the question, How can sinners possibly 40find acceptance and a restored relationship with a holy God41?
